Materials

Before diving into the concrete preparation, the initial step of the process entails selecting the materials and color schemes that will define your metallic epoxy flooring. While it is important to note that the highest grade materials may require licenses for purchase, there are still numerous options available for you to explore. Within the epoxy market, metallic epoxy is known by various names, such as Dazzle Flooring, Shimmer Floors, and Decorative Epoxies. It is crucial to carefully choose a material that offers the desired level of pigments and metallic shards, ensuring it aligns with your creative vision. Take the time to explore the available options and find the perfect combination that will infuse your flooring project with an enchanting allure.


Tools

Contrary to popular belief, the installation of a metallic pigmented flooring system involves more than simply mixing and pouring. There are several crucial steps that lie ahead. The next pivotal phase is the profiling of your concrete slab. It is important to note that the tools required for this process can be quite expensive, with a complete system costing thousands of dollars. Furthermore, without the appropriate equipment, the process can be labor-intensive and physically taxing. This is precisely why engaging the services of professional epoxy flooring contractors can prove to be a wise investment, saving you valuable time, money, and sparing you from aching muscles. The profiling tools necessary for this step include a grinder equipped with a diamond wheel blade, which will be connected to a vacuum system, as well as a pressure washer for thorough cleaning of the concrete slab. By enlisting the expertise of skilled professionals, you can ensure that the profiling process is executed with precision and efficiency, setting the stage for the flawless installation of your metallic epoxy flooring.

The preparation process for your concrete slab in anticipation of applying metallic epoxy is an intricate endeavor, involving multiple crucial elements. It is essential to ensure that your concrete meets specific parameters, including:

  • Moisture Minimization: To mitigate the risk of delamination, it is imperative to minimize moisture within the concrete substrate.

  • Profiling and Flatness: The concrete surface should be properly profiled, providing a textured and even substrate that can readily accept the epoxy coating.

  • Damage-Free Condition: Prior to application, it is vital to ascertain that the concrete slab is free from any damage, such as cracks or other structural imperfections.

By adhering to these parameters, you lay the groundwork for a successful installation of metallic epoxy. This meticulous preparation ensures a solid foundation for the application, enhancing the longevity and visual appeal of your finished flooring system.

Given the intricacy and meticulousness demanded by metallic epoxy installations, numerous homeowners choose to enlist the expertise of professional contractors to handle the preparation of the concrete slab, as well as the pouring and finishing of the epoxy flooring system. Recognizing the importance of achieving optimal results, it is prudent not to overlook the option of seeking professional assistance for this particular phase of the process. The skill and experience possessed by these specialists can prove invaluable, ensuring that every aspect of the installation is executed with precision and finesse. By entrusting the task to professionals, you can have peace of mind knowing that your metallic epoxy flooring project is in capable hands, resulting in a stunning and flawlessly executed final product.

Mixing The Epoxy

When embarking on the mixing of your epoxy flooring, it is crucial to carefully review the instructions provided by the materials manufacturer before even contemplating the blending process. Each material may have specific mixing durations and resting periods that must be adhered to prior to application onto the concrete slab. On average, the mixing and waiting times for epoxy flooring range between 5 to 10 minutes, contingent upon the specific materials employed. To commence the mixing procedure, it is recommended to combine both part A and part B of the epoxy into a standard five-gallon bucket, readily available at local hardware stores. To ensure thorough blending of the epoxy components, employing an electric drill equipped with a paint mixing paddle is highly advised, facilitating a comprehensive chemical reaction throughout the entire mixture. In the absence of an electric drill, a wooden paint stirrer can suffice for the task. Now, let us proceed to the next intricate step of the process.


Application


The application of your epoxy is undoubtedly an enjoyable step in the process, yet it also carries the risk of being the easiest to mishandle. Once your epoxy mixture is prepared and ready to be poured, it is crucial to initiate the application promptly, as epoxy is a time-sensitive material. For optimal results, we recommend utilizing a large squeegee to spread the epoxy evenly, achieving a thickness of approximately 2mm, which will yield stunning visual effects. During the application of the base coat of epoxy, consider incorporating some metallic pigments into the mixture, distributing them evenly to enhance the brilliance of the final finish. It is important to note that this is the stage where the expertise of professional contractors can prove invaluable. They possess the knowledge and have access to materials such as solvents, which can be employed to achieve finishes that may be challenging to replicate in a DIY project. Once you are satisfied with the appearance of your metallic epoxy, it is time to exercise patience and await the next phase of the process.

Curing

And now, we enter the waiting period. When it comes to installing epoxy flooring, it is essential to adhere to a general guideline: allow a minimum of 24 hours before walking on the freshly applied epoxy, and a minimum of 72 hours must elapse before utilizing the flooring as a regular part of your daily routine.
